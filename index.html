W8ER . com


Hi! I'm Larry and I live in Sandusky Ohio.

I grew up in Sandusky but moved to Chicago to work at a large radio station. A chance to work for IBM became available and I started a whole new career! Upon retiring I moved back to Sandusky for the "quiet life".

I have 2 hobbies, pc's and ham radio, and they keep me busy. Computers and Software Defined Radio's are the perfect marriage. I can be found most mornings on 80 meters, 3660 khz, having coffee and starting the day with some radio friends.

W8ER Station

I am using Flex 6400M transceiver with SmartSDR software. The 6400M drives an old Drake L4B amplifier. SmartSDR runs on a homebuilt Intel i9 computer using Windows 10.

A Electrovoice RE-320 drives a Symetrix 528e and that supplies the Flex with transmit audio. THe Flex sends receive audio to a Symetrix 420 20 watt amplifier and it drives a pair of JBL bookshelf speakers on a shelf right above my head.

Antennas

The 65 foot Rohn 25 tower is located 250 feet behind my house. I'm not fighting RF in the shack problems anymore. I have 2 runs of hardline running underground to the tower. The antennas are wire dipoles, each trimmed for the frequencies that I normally use.

Computers

The main desktop computer at W8ER is a homebrew Intel I9. It uses an Asus motherboard and has 32G of high speed RAM. For storage it is using plug in 500G SSDs. Typically I use Windows 10, but I can also plug in other drives from the front panel with other Operating Systems like Windows 11 and Linux Mint.

Intel NUC's

A computer that has fascinated me since it's introduction is the Intel NUC. I have several, doing various duties around the house. They are all networked together. NUC's are fascinating little computers that are able to keep up with the BIG guy and are very reliable. I have 6 of them here and all are very busy doing things like being the server hosting w8er.com!
